Copyright

Basic Copyright principles:

There are 6 different types of copyright licenses, four of which are:

  • CC BY:Credit must be given to creator

  • CC BY-SA:Credit must be given to creator & adaptations must be shared under same terms

  • CC BY-NC:Credit must be given to creator & only noncommercial uses of the work are permitted

  • CC BY-NC-SA:Combnation of all of the previous.

The Law:To safeguard literary creations, Congress passed the federal Copyright Act. A copyrighted work's owner has the sole authority to carry out specific actions with regard to it, including making copies, disseminating the work, and putting it on display or performing it in public. Public Domain Works:The public domain includes any works created and published before 1923. Since 1978, works have traditionally been protected by copyright for the duration of the author's life plus an additional 70 years. If the appropriate copyright procedures are followed, works published from 1923 to 1978 are protected for 95 years from the date of publication. Fair Use: A usage is not necessarily fair just because it has an educational or nonprofit purpose. If an usage advances, critiques, comments on, parodies, or otherwise contributes anything new to the original work, it is considered transformative. Is the new use simply "supercedes the objects of the original?"

Privacy & Web policy

A privacy policy is a written declaration that explains how a business gathers, manages, uses, and respects the personal information of its users on a website or mobile application. In order to guarantee that its clients or website visitors understand what personal data the firm gathers and how the company will use that information, the majority of privacy policies employ specific and unambiguous language.
